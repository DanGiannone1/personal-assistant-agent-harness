# Where the Work Lives

*A manifesto. The product doesn't have a name yet — good names follow real things, and we're
building the real thing first.*

---

**The problem is not that we lack an AI assistant. It's that our work is homeless.**

An engagement — the actual unit of our job — lives nowhere. It's a OneNote page, a health
tracker in Excel, four Teams threads, a deck assembled the night before the review, and a
status that exists mostly in one person's head. The AI assistants we use every day are
genuinely good, and they are places you *bring* work: you paste in the fragment, you get help,
and the moment the tab closes, the assistant remembers nothing and the state has moved on
without you.

A general assistant is where you bring your work. **We are building the place where the work
lives.**

---

## The one property we will not trade

When the assistant here says it moved a milestone, flagged a risk, or created an action item,
the screen renders from state the tools *actually changed* — never from what the model *claims*
it did. A claim can never outrun reality. Every tool answers honestly: it worked, it was a
no-op, or it failed — no theatrical success.

This sounds small. It's everything. It's the difference between an assistant you double-check
and an assistant you hand your engagement to. Chat products can't retrofit this; it has to be
the foundation. It's ours.

---

## What we're building

An agentic engagement workspace for people who do our job:

- **Your stuff** — tasks, notes, documents, reminders — private to you.
- **Our stuff** — engagements: health, milestones, risks, actions, artifacts — shared with the
  team that runs them.
- **An assistant that operates both** — not by summarizing screenshots, but by working the same
  system of record you see, with real memory of you, your team, and the engagement — because it
  lives where the data lives.

Ask it *"which of my engagements is drifting, and why?"* and the answer comes from records,
with receipts.

---

## Customer zero is us

We advise customers on agentic systems for a living. So we hold ourselves to the only honest
bar: **we run our own engagements in this tool.** If it can't survive our Monday morning, it
doesn't ship as our advice. And because we build it the way we'd tell a customer to —
identity-native auth, private data paths, serverless economics, no framework lock-in —
everything in it doubles as reference architecture we can hand across the table. We demo what
we live in. The accelerator is the product; the product is the accelerator.

---

## Principles

1. **The work lives here.** The assistant operates the system of record; it doesn't narrate
   about it.
2. **A claim never outruns reality.** UI renders from verified state. Tools report `ok`,
   `noop`, or `error` — never fiction.
3. **Deterministic where possible, model where valuable.** Navigation resolves by rules;
   judgment goes to the model. Don't spend intelligence on what logic can do.
4. **Decide, don't interrogate.** The agent makes the call and shows its work; it escalates
   only ambiguity that actually matters.
5. **Security is not a later.** Identity-native auth, private endpoints, managed identity,
   zero shared keys where the platform allows — from the first commit, because our customers'
   constraints are our constraints.
6. **Frugal is an architecture property.** Serverless, scale-to-zero, pay for thought — not
   for idle.
7. **No harness religion.** The agent runtime is swappable by design. The product is the
   invariants, not the framework of the month.
8. **Ship small, ship real, ship weekly.** A feature that survives one real engagement beats
   three that survive a demo.

---

## What this is not

- Not another chat wrapper. The chat is a control surface, not the product.
- Not a demo that dies after the review meeting.
- Not a replacement for the systems of record we don't own — it connects to them,
  consent-gated, and never pretends to be them.
- Not an attempt to out-model the model labs. Models are rented. Where the work lives is owned.

---

## The ask

Bring one real engagement into it. Use it for a week. Break it. Then tell us the first thing
you reached for that wasn't there — that list *is* the roadmap.

The bar we're chasing is simple: **you open it Monday morning without anyone asking you to.**
